The Problem We Exist to Address

A misallocation of attention and talent

Universities still have real strengths, but many of their best students and junior scholars experience them as more about bureaucracy, politics, and metrics than about understanding.

Historically, when a civilization changes its mind about itself, it does not do so because one person writes a book in a vacuum. It happens because scenes form.

The Avalon Institute

The French Enlightenment had its salons. The 19th-century Germans had Jena and Berlin. If existing universities cannot play that role now, we need an institution that will.

Our San Francisco Venue

Avalon House

Our house in Twin Peaks has become a kind of continuous, living salon. Since September 2024 we have hosted poetry readings, string quartets, performances of Platonic dialogues, evenings devoted to Nietzsche and Hildegard von Bingen.
